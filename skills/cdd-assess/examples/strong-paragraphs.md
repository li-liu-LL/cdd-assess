# Strong CDD Paragraph Examples

## Weak

The report notes that source of wealth is partially supported.

## Strong

The customer declares SGD 7,780,000 in net worth, mainly from property and employment income [S1 §4 field "Estimated Net Worth"]. Institution statements independently evidence SGD 920,000 through closing balances at ABC Bank and DEF Securities [S4 p.2 field "Closing Balance"; S5 p.1 field "Portfolio Value"]. The remaining SGD 6,860,000, or 88%, is not independently corroborated in the received materials after checking the onboarding form, bank statements, securities statement, employment letter, and tax declaration. This limits the reviewer’s ability to connect the declared accumulation to observed assets.

## Weak

No sanctions screening was provided.

## Strong

No sanctions, PEP/RCA, or adverse-media screening result is observed after checking the onboarding form screening section, the compliance checklist, all seven received source files, and filenames in `client-packs/001-individual-jane-tan/materials/`. The customer declaration states that the customer is not a PEP [S1 §5 field "PEP Declaration"], but a declaration is not a screening-system result. The absence affects the screening baseline rather than the customer identity facts.

## Weak

The ownership structure appears simple.

## Strong

ACME Pte Ltd is declared to be owned 60% by Lim Wei and 40% by Tan Mei [S2 p.3 table "Shareholders"]. The register extract independently records the same share counts and percentages for both natural-person shareholders [S3 row "Lim Wei"; S3 row "Tan Mei"]. No intermediate entity, nominee shareholder, bearer-share indicator, or trust arrangement is observed after checking the register extract, onboarding UBO declaration, board resolution, and all corporate materials. On the received evidence, the ownership chain reaches natural persons at the first layer, with identity evidence still assessed separately.
