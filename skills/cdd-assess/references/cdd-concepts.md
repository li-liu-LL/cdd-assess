# CDD Concepts

Use this reference when a report needs stronger customer-understanding discipline. It is self-contained methodology for CDD assessment reports, not legal advice and not a substitute for jurisdiction-specific regulatory research.

## Core View

CDD is not document collection. It is a structured enquiry that builds a customer risk profile by identifying the customer, understanding the relationship, verifying key information, and surfacing risk factors for human review.

Identification collects the data needed for the customer file. Verification tests whether that data is true, current, reliable, independent, and usable. Assessment observations should be grounded in that verified factual record.

## Customer Type First

Classify the customer before analysis:

- **Individual / private person**: a natural person. Build the record around identity, legal capacity, occupation, personal circumstances, financial position, objectives, source of wealth, source of funds, expected activity, and screening scope.
- **Corporate / legal entity**: a company or other legal arrangement. Build the record around legal form, jurisdiction, business activity, ownership/control, directors/controllers, public/regulated/listed status, and available registry or formation sources.
- **Unclear**: do not draft. Resolve whether the pack belongs to an individual, corporate, trust, fund, intermediary, or mixed case.

Customer type determines the factual record, evidence expectations, verification sources, derivations, and report sections.

## Individual Record

For individuals, structure facts under:

- identity: full name, aliases, date/place of birth, nationality, tax residency, identity document, address, contact details
- legal and practical capacity: age, authority to act, principal/agent status, bankruptcy or legal-capacity signals where relevant
- personal circumstances: occupation, employer/business, industry, residence history, education where relevant, family/dependants where relevant
- financial position and objectives: income, assets, liabilities, investment objectives, expected account use, expected transaction volume/frequency
- SOW/SOF: source of wealth as accumulated assets over time; source of funds as money entering a specific transaction or relationship
- monitoring baseline: expected pattern of activity for later comparison

Flag implausibility, inconsistency, non-face-to-face verification, high-net-worth source questions, PEP/RCA exposure, and gaps in current or independent evidence.

## Corporate Record

For corporates and legal arrangements, structure facts under:

- legal form and jurisdiction: company, partnership, trust, foundation, charity, fund, SPV, PIV, regulated firm, listed entity, government body
- formation and registry evidence: certificate, articles/constitution, trust deed, partnership agreement, registry extract, licences, annual return, audited accounts
- business activity: actual activity, countries, products/services, counterparties, rationale for relationship
- ownership/control: shareholders, indirect owners, beneficial owners, controllers, directors, trustees, nominees, bearer-share indicators, senior managing officials
- lower-risk indicators: listed, regulated, or public status only when the listing/regulation is credible and effective
- reliance: note when another regulated party supplies CDD comfort, but preserve responsibility and limits

Flag legal forms or structures that obscure ownership/control, create complexity without commercial rationale, or require private-person-style enquiries behind the entity.

## Verification Discipline

Verification assesses whether facts are reliable enough to support the report. Record:

- source type: government, regulator/registry, audited financials, regulated institution, customer representation, public source, vendor, internal record
- independence from the customer
- currency/freshness
- legibility and language/translation status
- certification or authentication status where relevant
- confidence and limits

Reliable, current, independent sources carry more weight than customer representations. Customer representations can still be useful, but mark them as non-independent and explain what would corroborate them.

## Source Reliability Ladder

Use this generic ladder unless the compliance knowledge pack defines a stricter one:

1. Government, court, regulator, registry, or official identity source.
2. Regulated institution or audited professional source.
3. Independent commercial source with clear provenance.
4. Customer-provided document or representation.
5. Inference, estimate, or model extraction.

Never collapse these levels into one confidence label. A fact can be accurately extracted from a weak source; record both extraction confidence and source reliability.

## SOW, SOF, Net Worth, And Plausibility

Treat SOW/SOF as customer-understanding tools, not form labels:

- **SOW**: how wealth/assets were accumulated over time.
- **SOF**: where the specific money for the transaction or relationship comes from.
- **Net worth corroboration**: reconcile declared assets, liabilities, income, bank/investment evidence, business proceeds, inheritance, property sale, and other support.

When estimating or deriving values, show source inputs, assumptions, method, confidence, and unresolved gaps.

## Ownership And Control

Beneficial ownership analysis is an unwrapping exercise. Identify natural persons who ultimately own or control the customer, including indirect or aggregate holdings across branches. Ownership below a numeric threshold may still matter where combined with control, family/associate links, nominee arrangements, bearer shares, or senior-management authority.

For the report, present ownership/control as a visual map plus a table of parties, roles, percentages, sources, confidence, and verification status.

## Relationship Purpose And Monitoring Baseline

Capture why the customer wants the relationship, what products/services are expected, and what transaction pattern is anticipated. This baseline helps future reviewers compare actual activity against the customer's stated purpose and profile.

Where the material pack lacks expected activity details, record the gap rather than inventing a baseline.

## Report Implications

- Put customer type and customer name in folder and report filenames.
- Separate raw extracted facts from verified facts and analytical derivations.
- Make uncertainty visible instead of smoothing it away.
- Put exhibits near the facts they support.
- Treat facts, derivations, and observations as review-ready inputs, not decisions.
