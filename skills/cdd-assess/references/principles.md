# Principles

Use this reference when shaping the tone, structure, and judgment discipline of a CDD report. These principles translate official CDD expectations into report craft without importing jurisdiction-specific thresholds.

## Mission

Produce an internal compliance report that helps a reviewer understand the customer, the evidence, the unresolved uncertainty, and the regulatory relevance without reconstructing the onboarding file.

## Grounding Principles

### Risk-based, not checklist-based

Use checklists to prevent omissions, but do not let them replace judgment. A CDD report should help the reviewer understand why the facts matter and whether the evidence is proportionate to the customer's risk profile. FATF frames the risk-based approach as identifying, assessing, and understanding risk, then applying proportionate controls.

### Customer understanding is the core product

The report should answer: who is the customer, what do they do, who owns or controls them, why do they need the relationship, what activity is expected, and what evidence supports those answers. FATF Recommendation 10 grounds CDD in identification, verification, beneficial ownership, purpose and intended nature, and ongoing understanding of the customer profile.

### Decision-readiness, not decision-making

Official CDD regimes expect records that support decisions, monitoring, and supervisory review. This skill should make the file decision-ready by surfacing facts, gaps, conflicts, confidence, and observations. It should not recommend approval, rejection, escalation, conditions, or operational actions.

### Evidence quality is part of the fact

Do not present a fact without also making its source and reliability inspectable. Distinguish collected, verified, inferred, estimated, and low-confidence facts. Where source of funds, source of wealth, net worth, or beneficial ownership requires derivation, show the inputs, assumptions, method, confidence, and human-verification status.

### Missing evidence is information

Never smooth over missing, stale, contradictory, or unverifiable information. Make uncertainty visible in the factual record and gaps section. A clean-looking report with hidden uncertainty is less useful than a precise report that names what remains unresolved.

### Portable means source-layered

Do not hard-code local regulatory thresholds or procedures as universal rules. Treat FATF-style concepts as baseline principles, then layer jurisdiction, regulator, entity policy, product, and client-type requirements from the compliance knowledge pack or fresh primary-source research.

### Presentation is a control

Readable structure, citations, exhibits, tables, charts, and version history reduce reviewer error and make the work auditable. Beauty is not decoration here; it is the discipline of making complex evidence inspectable under time pressure.

## Sources To Prefer

- FATF recommendations, guidance, and glossary for international baseline concepts.
- Official regulator, statute, FIU, government, court, or supervisory material for jurisdiction-specific requirements.
- Recognized standards bodies where they are the relevant benchmark.
- Internal policies and procedures for entity-specific overlays.

Use secondary sources only to discover primary sources or clarify terminology. Do not use them as final authority.
